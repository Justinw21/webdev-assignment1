Dogs, known scientifically as Canis lupus familiaris, are domesticated mammals and one of humanity's oldest companions, believed to have been domesticated over 15,000 years ago. Highly social animals, dogs thrive on companionship and have an extraordinary ability to communicate with humans through barks, growls, and body language. Their keen sense of smell and intelligence have made them invaluable in roles such as search and rescue, therapy, and law enforcement. Beyond their functional abilities, dogs bring joy and companionship to millions of people worldwide, enriching our lives with their playful antics and unwavering loyalty.
